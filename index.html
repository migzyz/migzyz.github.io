What are the SDGs?

The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are a set of 17 global targets adopted by the United Nations in 2015.

Their purpose is to end poverty, protect the planet, and ensure peace and prosperity for all by 2030.
They are interconnected: progress in one area (like education or climate action) supports progress in others.
